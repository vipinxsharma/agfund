About AGFUND

The Arab Gulf Programme for Development (AGFUND) is a multilateral development finance institution founded in 1980 to support sustainable human development and poverty alleviation. It focuses on vulnerable groups (women, children, people with disabilities, displaced persons) to enable dignified social/economic lives. AGFUND aligns with UN SDGs 2030, emphasizing education, financial inclusion, and innovation.

History and Founding

Founded on the initiative of HRH Prince Talal bin Abdulaziz with support from Arab Gulf leaders (Kuwait City meeting, April 21, 1980). Key milestones: 1997 microfinance initiative; 1999 launch of International Prize.

"Human being is human being wherever exists" – Prince Talal

Mission, Vision, and Strategy

Mission: Support sustainable human development by identifying successful experiences, networking partners, and providing grants/equity for projects benefiting target groups.

Vision: A world where vulnerable groups exercise civil rights, lead dignified lives, and integrate into production processes via education, skills, and financial inclusion.
